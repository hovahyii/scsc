About The Event

About The Event

Get started with one of the world’s most extensible programming languages that is also one of the easiest to pick up. Widely used for robotics, computer games, Artificial Intelligence and Internet of Things, Python emphasizes on easy to read codes and leverages on the vast variety of available Open Source Libraries to achieve complex operations with minimal effort. This workshop is geared for students who are both new programmers and seasoned ones eager to get started with Python.

Speaker Details

SCSC Club Advisor and Swinburne Lecturer.

Dr. Mark Tee Kit Tsun

Dr. Mark Tee received his BSc(Hons) in Computer Science from Coventry University in 2005 and served in the software development industry until 2008. He completed a Masters in Software Engineering (OUM) and a BEng(Hons) from Swinburne University of Technology Sarawak by 2014. He completed his PhD candidature at the same university in 2018 and joined the Faculty of Engineering, Computing and Science as a Lecturer in the following year.

His previous research areas include computer game development, drone technology applications and assistive robotics for injury prevention.

Dr. Mark Tee has been SCSC advisor since 2018 December. Since then, he has been actively organising events and activities for SCSC while also involving himself in IEEE and Swinburne Robotics and Automation Club.

F.A.Q

  • Is this a free workshop?

    Yes, the admission is free, which means you do not need to pay anything.

  • Who can join this workshop?

    This workshop is open for secondary school students in Kuching only. If you are Swinburne student, you can join as volunteer or helper.

  • How many students can join this workshop?

    This workshop is only open for 30 students, the seats are limited. Register before 20 November to secure the spot. Registration closed on 20 November or when the spot is fully occupied.

  • Do I need to know programming or have any computer science background to join this workshop?

    Nope, this workshop is intended for beginner. No prerequisite or background needed. Just come and learn.

  • What should I prepare or bring for this workshop?

    You can bring your own laptop, but there are computers for you to use at the venue as well. Sleep well before the event day. Be attentive and proactive during the event is all you need.

  • Who organises the event?

    This event is solely organised by Swinburne Sarawak Computer Science Club(SCSC). The invited speaker is the SCSC club advisor.

  • What can I learn by joining this event?

    You will learn about Python, the most popular programming language and also some useful programming knowledge and basics.

  • Whom can I contact to learn more about this event?

    You can directly contact Dr. Mark Tee, the speaker for this event. The contact number is shown at the bottom.
